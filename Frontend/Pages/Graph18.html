Graph Explanation

The top ten North American nations with the most airline passengers are depicted in the horizontal bar chart. Every horizontal bar stands for a particular nation, and the number of passengers from that nation is indicated by the length of the bar. The number of passengers from that country increases with the length of the bar. The graphic presents a clear picture of the distribution of air travel in North America by comparing the passenger counts of these nations visually. It is easy to understand the relative importance of each country in terms of air travel within the continent when using this type of visualization, which is effective in identifying the countries that contribute the most to the overall passenger traffic.
